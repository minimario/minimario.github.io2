some brief thoughts on decision making

- it’s interesting when we try to notice all of the (smallest) decisions we’re making in our lives and overthink all of them

- basing life decisions off of the pareto principle maximizes some sense of value

- the time it takes to make a decision is time out of your life

- a lot of “hard decisions” don’t matter either way and more time is not going to increase your capability of making a better decision

- asking other people to help you make decisions increases trust both ways

- if you’re choosing from a finite number of options, maybe you’re not being creative enough

- wisdom of crowds is a nice framework for decision making

- cost-benefit analysis is usually too impractical and inaccurate

- the value of emotions is very hard to judge, it’s important to make decisions when not being super emotional but also being able to factor emotions into a decision is important

- some type of “stakeholder analysis” but pretending you’re all the stakeholders gives you a lot of perspective

- gut feeling is surprisingly useful and looking into your body’s reactions to a decision gives you a lot of information on possible emotional outcomes

- i tend to be quite optimistic when i map out the outcomes of a decision, so i’m more likely to make higher variance decisions if i perceive them to be similar in value

- the longer i think about a decision, the less intimate it feels

- there are probably trends associated with the “goodness” of a decision vs. things like time since last meal, time of day, happiness, emotional amplitude, etc.; might be worth thinking about these trends

- if you make some number of decisions in a row, the 1st one usually isn’t the “best” one
